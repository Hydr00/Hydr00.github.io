What is password fatigue

When you enter a password, you feel tired and tired, which is psychologically called password fatigue.

Here are some typical episodes of password fatigue：

Request to create a new password; the password created is too simple to meet the requirements of the website and requires re-creation; when you create a password, you are required to enter it twice; when you have logged in, but when you enter an important function, you are required to enter the password again. When you create a password, you don't display or display placeholders, and you can't see what you're entering at all.

Generation of password fatigue

There are many passwords in the network age. Computer boot password, access email password, login QQ password, blog password, forum message password, online stock trading password, online shopping password, online game password, use bank card password, The password is required to turn on the mobile phone.……

For security, people not only have to set a lot of passwords, but also change passwords from time to time. As a result, people have to carry out a large number of password memory, fall into the trap of password fatigue. However, people have been or are afraid of suffering from password fatigue, and do not fasten the password "fence", resulting in security loopholes, resulting in losses.

Many people have experienced "password fatigue", remember a large number of passwords have become a part of people's daily life, such as computer login password at work, unlock password, ATM operation password and so on. This is also known as "password confusion" or "ID chaos". Information technology and the Internet are becoming more and more important in work, finance, leisure and other aspects of life, and the introduction of secure trading technology has enabled people to accumulate a large number of accounts and passwords.

A crowd of password fatigue

Password fatigue is a high incidence in enterprises. Patient employees usually look the same as ordinary people, but they have specific rejection of passwords. As soon as they see the password input box, their brains are emptied, and the password is forgotten. But they came up with a good solution, and that is to use a simple password, the simpler the better, what 123456, name, birthday, how to remember, or simply use the default password for thousands of years, root, admin, and so on.

A survey conducted in the UK in 2002 showed that the average Internet user had 21 accounts per person. By now, that number is likely to have doubled and is likely to surpass that of the United States. Almost all Web portals require users to sign up for a new account and remember their account password to ensure the "security" of their personal accounts. These endless demands wear us out, and the end result is "password fatigue."
